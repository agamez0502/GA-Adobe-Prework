Terrifier 3 (2024)

Listen, Art the Clown is back and somehow creepier than ever. This one leans into chaotic gore but also tries giving a *Christmas horror* twist. It's over-the-top, unsettling, and honestly kind of hilarious at times. Not for the faint of heart, but if you love campy carnage - it's a bloody good time.

Together (2025)

This one surprised me - creepy psychological undertones wrapped in what looks like a romantic drama at first. The way it unravels kept me hooked, and I caught myself yelling "girl don't do it" at the screen like 10 times. A slower burn, but the payoff? Chilling.

Tusk (2014)

Tusk is honestly an experience. Equal parts disturbing and absurd, it's Kevin Smith going fully unhinged. The body horror had me squirming (in a fun way?) and that walrus suit is now permanently stuck in my brain. It's bizarre, it's messed up, and yes, I kind of loved it.
